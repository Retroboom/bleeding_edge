YOUR TURN: THREE PHASES

turn_sequence.exe

Every turn follows the same structure:

Phase 1: START

  1. Lose remaining Bandwidth (it doesn't carry over)
  2. Add 1 Loading counter to each of your Cached cards
  3. Refresh all your cards (rotate them upright)
  4. Resolve start-of-turn effects (if any cards trigger)

Phase 2: ACTION

You gain 3 Bandwidth‚Äîyour resource for this turn.

Spend it on these actions (in any order, any number of times):

  • Draw a Card ‚Üí Cost: 1 Bandwidth
  • Deploy a Card ‚Üí Cost: Card's deployment cost
  • Make a Play ‚Üí Cost: 1 Bandwidth (attack opponent's card)

Phase 3: END

  1. Remove 1 Disruption from each of your cards
  2. Resolve end-of-turn effects (if any)
  3. Discard down to 7 cards (if over hand limit)

DEPLOYING CARDS

deploy.sys

To deploy a card:

  1. Pay at least 1 Bandwidth (unless the card costs 0)
  2. Pay the rest with additional Bandwidth OR by discarding cards from hand
  3. Check the Loyalty requirement

Payment Rules

When discarding cards to pay costs:

  • Discard 1 card = 1 Bandwidth equivalent
  • Cards must match faction (Takahashi cards pay for Takahashi, etc.)
  • Neutral cards can pay for anything
  • Allied faction cards can pay for each other (if you declared an Alliance)

The Loyalty System

Cards have Loyalty values (L0, L1, L2, etc.) that determine when you can deploy them:

LOYALTY REQUIREMENT
L0 None‚Äîdeploy anytime
L1 Need 1 card of that faction already on the board
L2 Need 2 cards of that faction already on the board
L3 Need 3 cards of that faction already on the board
üéØ CRITICAL: Only face-up deployed cards count for Loyalty. Cached (face-down) cards DO NOT count.

Example: To deploy an L2 Takahashi Agent, you need 2 Takahashi cards already in play.

Start with L0 cards, then build up to deploy your powerful high-Loyalty cards!

MAKING PLAYS: THE CORE OF COMBAT

make_play.exe

A Play is how you attack your opponent's cards. There are four types, one for each Method.

How to Make a Play

  1. Pay 1 Bandwidth
  2. Choose a target (opponent's Agent or Asset)
  3. Declare the Method (Violence, Influence, Intrusion, or Override)
  4. Commit your Agents to the Play (rotate them sideways)
  5. Counter Phase - Either player can play Exploits
  6. Opponent declares blockers (commits Agents to defend)
  7. Counter Phase - Either player can play Exploits
  8. Compare totals and resolve

Targeting Rules (Important!)

CARD STATE CAN BE TARGETED BY
Committed (rotated sideways) ALL METHODS
Uncommitted (upright) INTRUSION ONLY
Protected üõ° INTRUSION ONLY
Cached (face-down) üíæ INTRUSION ONLY
Has Backdoor marker üö™ ALL METHODS (Backdoor removes all restrictions!)
üéØ CRITICAL TARGETING RULE: Uncommitted cards (upright, not rotated) can ONLY be targeted by Intrusion Plays. Violence, Influence, and Override can ONLY target committed (sideways) cards OR cards with Backdoor markers.
This is the #1 rule beginners forget! Intrusion is the universal Method. It can target ANYTHING‚Äîcommitted, uncommitted, Protected, or Cached cards.

>>> KEYWORD DEFINITIONS... LOADING STATUS EFFECTS <<<

KEYWORDS

keywords.db

Disrupted

A Disrupted card can't commit or use abilities. Remove 1 Disruption during your End Phase.

Protected

A Protected card can only be targeted by Intrusion Plays. (Unless it has a Backdoor marker.)

Commit

Rotate a card sideways to use it for a Play, block, or activate a "Commit //" ability. Committed cards refresh (rotate upright) during your Start Phase.

>>> ADVANCED PROTOCOLS UNLOCKED... CACHING SUBSYSTEM INITIALIZED <<<

CACHED CARDS (ADVANCED)

cache_protocol.exe

Instead of deploying face-up, you can Cache a card:

  • Pay 1 Bandwidth
  • Place card face-down
  • Add 1 Loading counter

Each Start Phase, Cached cards gain another Loading counter.

Revealing

During your Action Phase, reveal the card, pay remaining cost (original cost minus Loading counters), and deploy it. Some cards have Loading effects that trigger when revealed with enough counters.

üéØ REMEMBER: Cached cards DO NOT count for Loyalty requirements! Only face-up deployed cards count.

Cracking

If an opponent successfully targets your Cached card with Intrusion, it's revealed. You can pay to deploy it, or it's trashed.

Why Cache?

  • Surprise opponent (hidden information)
  • Delay payment (pay over multiple turns)
  • Build Loyalty threshold for high-loyalty cards
  • Trigger Loading effects (rewards patience)

DECK EXHAUSTION

If you need to draw and your deck is empty:
  1. Shuffle your trash (discard pile)
  2. Remove the top 10 cards from the game (hidden)
  3. The rest becomes your new deck

If you can't remove 10 cards and need to shuffle again, you lose the game.
